Receiving International Payments from India

If you work with international clients, your payment options are:

  • Wise (formerly TransferWise): Local bank accounts in USD, GBP, EUR. No conversion fee on receiving. Best for freelancers with regular international income.
  • Payoneer: Popular on Upwork/Fiverr. Annual fee ₹3,500. Good for marketplace withdrawals.
  • PayPal: High conversion fees (4–5%). Avoid unless client insists. Freeze risk on large amounts.
  • SWIFT wire transfer: Free to receive (bank charges vary ₹500–₹1,500). Get a FIRA (Foreign Inward Remittance Advice) from your bank — needed for GST zero-rating proof.
  • Flutterwave / Stripe Atlas: For freelancers who need a USD business account (requires setup).
FIRA reminder: Always get a FIRA letter from your bank for every international payment. It's your proof that the money was received for an export of services — required if you file for GST refund on zero-rated supplies.

Tax-saving tip: Under Section 44ADA (presumptive taxation), if your gross receipts are under ₹75 lakh, you can declare 50% of gross receipts as profit — without maintaining detailed books. This massively simplifies accounting. Read our ITR filing guide for how this works.

5. Communication & Meetings

Professional communication tools signal credibility. Use a custom email domain if possible (₹500/yr on Google Workspace or Zoho Mail free).

  • Gmail + Google Workspace: ₹125/user/month for custom domain email (yourname@yourdomain.com). Worth it once you're charging ₹30,000+ per project.
  • Zoho Mail: Free for 1 user with custom domain. Good starter option.
  • Google Meet: Free for 60-minute meetings. Standard for most Indian clients.
  • Calendly free tier: One event type (e.g., "30-min discovery call"). Share link instead of back-and-forth scheduling.
  • Loom: Record screen walkthroughs to explain work to clients. Free up to 25 videos. Reduces calls dramatically.
  • WhatsApp Business: Free. Set up a business profile with your services listed. Most Indian clients prefer WhatsApp for quick communication.

7. Portfolio & Personal Branding

Your portfolio is your 24/7 sales rep. You don't need to build a custom website immediately — use a free hosted option first and upgrade once clients are paying.

  • Behance / Dribbble: Free for designers and visual creatives. Standard platform Indian design clients check first.
  • Contra: Portfolio + proposals + payments for creative freelancers. Free tier.
  • LinkedIn: Non-negotiable for B2B freelancers. Optimize your headline and "About" section with keywords. See our finding clients guide for exact templates.
  • Carrd: ₹1,500/yr for a clean single-page portfolio site. Fast to set up.
  • Framer / Webflow: $14–$16/mo for custom portfolio. Worth it for designers who want to showcase their work at that level.
  • GitHub: For developers — your contribution graph and pinned repos are your portfolio.

When to Upgrade

Pay for tools only when the cost is offset by time saved or revenue unlocked:

  • Custom email domain: Once you're charging ₹20,000+ per project (₹125/mo Workspace)
  • Mitti Pro: Once you're generating 5+ documents per month (₹299/mo)
  • Vyapar: Once GST registered (₹83/mo)
  • Calendly paid: Rarely needed — free tier covers solo freelancers
  • Claude Pro / ChatGPT Plus: Once you use AI daily for client work ($20/mo via international card)
Bottom line: Indian freelancers tend to over-invest in tools and under-invest in skills and positioning. ₹0 tools + great work + professional proposals will beat ₹5,000/mo tool stack + mediocre proposals every time. Start free, upgrade when the ROI is obvious.
