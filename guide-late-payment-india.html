The Follow-Up Timeline

Most late payments resolve with polite persistence. Here's the escalation ladder:

1
Invoice due date + 1 day
Polite reminder email

Assume the invoice was forgotten. Keep it friendly. Re-attach the invoice.

2
Due date + 7 days
Second reminder — add urgency

Mention that work on current projects may need to pause until payment is received.

3
Due date + 14 days
Phone call + formal notice

Call the primary contact AND their manager or finance person if possible. Follow up in writing the same day.

4
Due date + 30 days
Legal notice via lawyer

Send a formal lawyer's notice. This costs ₹1,000–3,000 and resolves most disputes immediately.

5
Due date + 45–60 days
MSME Council / Consumer Court / Civil suit

Formal legal action. Use for amounts worth pursuing — typically ₹50,000+.

Legal Options in India

1. MSME Facilitation Council (Best for Registered MSMEs)

If you are registered as an MSME (Udyam Registration), you have a powerful tool: the MSME Facilitation Council. Under the MSMED Act 2006:

  • Buyers must pay within 45 days of accepting goods/services (or as agreed in writing, maximum 45 days)
  • Interest on delayed payment is 3× the RBI bank rate (currently ~18–22% per annum) — compounded monthly
  • You can file a complaint with the MSME Facilitation Council in your state
  • The Council provides conciliation and arbitration — faster and cheaper than civil courts
Udyam Registration is free and takes 10 minutes at udyamregistration.gov.in. Once registered, all payments to you are subject to MSMED Act protections — the interest penalty alone is often enough to motivate payment.

2. Consumer Forum (For Individuals or Small Businesses)

If your client is an individual or small business, you can file a complaint at the District Consumer Forum for amounts up to ₹1 crore. Filing fee is minimal (₹100–200), and cases often resolve in 3–6 months.

3. Section 138 — Dishonoured Cheque

If the client gave you a post-dated cheque that bounced, file a criminal complaint under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act within 30 days of receiving the bank's dishonour memo. This is a criminal offence carrying up to 2 years imprisonment — it's a very effective recovery tool.

4. Civil Suit (Money Recovery Suit)

For larger amounts (₹1 lakh+), you can file a civil suit in the appropriate civil court. The process is slow (1–3 years) but you can also seek an attachment order to freeze client assets. Use a lawyer for this.

5. Lawyer's Notice (Most Common First Step)

A formal lawyer's notice costs ₹1,000–5,000 and resolves 70% of disputed invoices immediately. It signals you're serious. Draft it with your actual lawyer to reference the specific contract, GST invoice number, and applicable interest rate.

How to Avoid Late Payments Next Time

  • Always take 50% advance before starting work. Non-negotiable for new clients.
  • Milestone billing: For long projects, bill monthly or by milestone — don't complete everything before billing.
  • Net-15 not Net-30: Standard in India is Net-30 but you can negotiate Net-15 — especially for repeat clients.
  • Late payment clause in contract: Add 1.5–2% per month interest on overdue amounts. Even if you never enforce it, it accelerates payment.
  • Withhold final deliverables: Don't deliver the final design files / source code until final payment is received. Keep a "watermarked preview" policy.
  • Annual retainers paid quarterly: For retainer clients, bill quarterly in advance instead of monthly. Reduces 8× invoices to 4×.
  • UPI / NEFT before delivery: "I'll send the final files as soon as I see the transfer." Most clients respect this once it's established upfront.
