8 Sections Every Video Editing Proposal Needs

1
Project Overview & Video Brief

Type of video (YouTube long-form, Instagram Reels, brand film, ad/commercial, corporate presentation, event highlight reel), total number of videos, approximate duration of each, raw footage provided by client or shot by you, platform(s) for distribution.

2
Deliverables & Specifications

Be precise about what you'll deliver:

Also specify: colour grading (included or extra), subtitles/captions (included or extra), thumbnail design (included or extra), music licensing (client-sourced or you'll provide royalty-free).

3
Revision Policy

This is where video editors lose money. Be specific:

  • Round 1 (rough cut): Major structural changes allowed
  • Round 2 (fine cut): Minor timing, colour, text adjustments
  • Round 3 (final): Small corrections only (typos, audio levels)
  • Additional revision rounds: ₹X per round
  • Revisions must be submitted as a single consolidated document (not drip-fed)
Critical clause: Add "New creative direction after approval of rough cut constitutes a new project and will be quoted separately." This prevents clients who approved a concept from changing the entire storyline in Round 3.
4
Raw Footage & Assets

Specify client responsibilities clearly:

  • Client to provide all raw footage, images, and brand assets by [date]
  • Footage must be transferred via Google Drive / WeTransfer — not WhatsApp (quality loss)
  • If client footage is unusable quality, you reserve the right to request reshoot or adjust scope
  • Music: client to provide licensed tracks OR you'll source from Epidemic Sound/Artlist (cost included or billed separately)
  • Project start is contingent on receipt of all assets
5
Licensing & Ownership

For video, licensing matters enormously:

  • Standard licence: Client can use the final video on specified platforms for personal/commercial use. You retain the right to use it in your portfolio.
  • Exclusive licence: Client gets exclusive rights — no portfolio use by you. Charge premium (2–3×).
  • Broadcast/TV licence: Requires separate licensing agreement and significantly higher fee.
  • Project files: Premiere Pro / DaVinci project files are not included by default. If client wants editable files, quote separately.
6
Timeline & Milestones

Typical timeline for a 5–10 minute YouTube video:

  • Day 1: Assets received, project kickoff confirmed
  • Day 5–7: Rough cut delivered for review
  • Day 9–11: Fine cut after first revision round
  • Day 13–15: Final delivery after second revision round
7

Retainers should be paid monthly in advance. Include a 30-day cancellation clause.

8
Pricing & GST

Video editing services fall under SAC 998392 (audiovisual and related services). GST @ 18% applies. For corporate clients, TDS @ 10% under Section 194J is deducted on the taxable value.
