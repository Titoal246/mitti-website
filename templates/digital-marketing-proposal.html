The 9 Sections Every Digital Marketing Proposal Needs

1
Situation Analysis

Brief audit of client's current digital presence: website traffic (if shared), Google rankings for 3–5 target keywords, social media following, and ad account history. This shows you've done homework and establishes the baseline against which you'll report results.

Why it matters: Clients who see you understand their current situation are far more likely to trust your recommendations.
2
Objectives & KPIs

Digital marketing is results-driven. Specify measurable goals — don't leave these vague. Use SMART metrics:

SEO
Page 1 rankings for 5 target keywords in 6 months
Paid Ads
Cost per lead below ₹300, ROAS ≥ 3× in 90 days
Social
15% engagement rate, +500 followers/month
Email
Open rate ≥ 28%, click rate ≥ 4%
Always distinguish between vanity metrics (reach, impressions) and business metrics (leads, sales, revenue attributed).
3
Scope of Services

Be explicit about what's included and excluded. Common digital marketing services:

ServiceTypical InclusionsCommon Exclusions
SEOOn-page optimisation, technical audit, monthly reporting, 4 blogs/monthPaid link building, website rebuild
Google Ads (PPC)Campaign setup, ad copy, weekly optimisation, monthly reportAd spend (billed separately), landing page design
Meta AdsCampaign setup, creative brief, A/B testing, reportingAd spend, video production
Social Media8–12 posts/month, captions, scheduling, engagement repliesPaid ads management, influencer sourcing
4
Ad Spend Separation (Critical for Paid Campaigns)

This is the most important section for paid media freelancers. Always separate your management fee from ad spend:

  • Your fee: Management, strategy, reporting — subject to GST @ 18%
  • Ad spend: Goes directly to Google/Meta — client pays this separately (not through you if possible)
  • If you collect and pass on ad spend, clarify it's a reimbursement and document with invoices
Why it matters: If ad spend goes through your account, GST and TDS calculations get complicated. Have clients pay Google/Meta directly under their billing accounts whenever possible.
5
Reporting & Communication

Specify: monthly report format (PDF/Google Data Studio/Looker Studio), what metrics are tracked, report delivery date (e.g., first 5 days of each month), review call cadence (monthly video call), and response time for queries (within 24 hours on working days).

6
Engagement Model: Project vs Retainer

Most digital marketing work is retainer-based. Options to present:

ModelPricingBest For
Monthly retainer (fixed scope)Set monthly fee for agreed deliverablesSEO, social media, content
% of ad spend10–20% of total ad spend managedPaid ads (Google, Meta)
Performance-basedBase retainer + bonus per lead/saleLead gen campaigns with clear attribution
Project-basedFixed fee for a defined projectAudits, campaign launches, one-time setups
7
Access & Approvals Required

List what you need from the client before work can begin:

  • Google Search Console and Google Analytics access (Editor role)
  • Google Ads account access (Standard access minimum)
  • Meta Business Manager access (Admin or Advertiser)
  • Website CMS access (if on-page SEO is in scope)
  • Brand assets: logo, brand guidelines, approved image library
  • Content approval turnaround: maximum 48 hours for client review
Delays in getting access or approvals delay your deliverables — document this as a client responsibility.
8
Timeline & Onboarding

Typical onboarding timeline for a full-service digital marketing retainer:

  • Week 1: Access setup, audit, baseline documentation
  • Week 2: Strategy document + content calendar approval
  • Week 3: Campaign/content goes live
  • Month 1 end: First performance report + strategy review
9
Pricing, GST & Payment Terms

Break out pricing clearly. Include GST at 18% (SAC 998361 for marketing/advertising services, SAC 998313 for technical/SEO work). Require advance payment before campaign launch.

Add TDS clause: "TDS @ 10% under Section 194J applicable on the taxable value if client is a company or firm. TDS not applicable on GST component."

Your proposal needs to clearly articulate what they get for the premium — dedicated attention, custom strategy, transparent reporting — and quantify the expected business impact (leads, revenue) not just traffic.
