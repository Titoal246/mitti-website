Licensing: The Most Overlooked Section

Most Indian design clients assume they own everything the moment they pay. That's not how IP law works — and it's not how you should price either. License scope affects price:

License TypeCoveragePrice Multiplier
Single-use digitalOne platform, one campaign1x base price
Full digitalAll digital channels, perpetual1.5x
Print + digitalAll digital + print media2x
Full commercialAll media + merchandise + sub-licensing3x
Buyout (source files)Everything including source files3.5–5x
Source files clause: Never hand over source files (AI, PSD, Figma source) as part of the standard deliverable. Source files = ability to make unlimited edits and remove your credit. Price source file handover separately — typically 50–100% on top of the design fee.

Revision Round Best Practices

Unlimited revisions are the fastest way to kill your profitability. Two rounds covers 95% of legitimate design feedback. Here's how to enforce it professionally:

  1. Get written feedback. Don't accept "I don't like it" on a call. Send a feedback form or ask them to comment on the shared file. Written feedback is clearer and creates a paper trail.
  2. Confirm direction before executing. After sharing concepts, ask: "Which direction would you like to proceed with?" Get a written reply before starting revisions.
  3. Flag scope creep early. If the client asks for something outside the agreed scope during revisions, reply: "That's a new deliverable — happy to quote it separately." Do this politely but immediately.
  4. Bill the third revision at the start. If a client is heading into a third revision round, raise the invoice before doing the work. It's much harder to get paid for extra work after it's delivered.

What to Include in Your Brand Identity Handoff

  • Logo files: AI/SVG (vector), PNG transparent background (multiple sizes), JPG on white background
  • Colour palette: HEX, RGB, CMYK, Pantone (if print) for each colour
  • Typography: font names, where to download (free/paid), license type (commercial use)
  • Pattern/texture files (if applicable)
  • Brand guidelines PDF — at minimum: logo rules, colour codes, typography, don'ts
  • Editable templates (business card, letterhead) in Canva or PowerPoint for client to reuse
