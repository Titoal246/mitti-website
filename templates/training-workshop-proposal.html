Pricing Models for Training: Which to Use When

ModelHow It WorksBest For
Flat programme feeFixed amount for the entire programme regardless of participantsSmall groups (under 30), in-house workshops
Per-head pricingFixed amount per participant (e.g., ₹1,200/person)Large groups (50+), public workshops, online cohorts
Day rateFixed daily fee regardless of content or participantsLong engagements, facilitation support, train-the-trainer
RetainerMonthly fee for a set number of days/sessionsOngoing L&D partnership, coaching programmes
Customisation fee is non-negotiable: Always charge separately for programme design and customisation — even if small. Clients who ask for a "small tweak" to the standard programme often end up requesting a completely custom curriculum. A design fee sets the expectation that customisation has value and creates a clear scope boundary.

What to Include in Post-Programme Deliverables

A post-programme report differentiates professional trainers from those who just show up and facilitate. Include:

  • Participant feedback summary (scores + selected verbatims)
  • Key themes from the programme (what resonated, what challenges came up)
  • Individual commitment actions (anonymised aggregate)
  • Facilitator observations: energy, engagement level, group dynamics
  • 3–5 recommended next steps for the organisation (follow-up coaching, practice reinforcement, manager enablement)
